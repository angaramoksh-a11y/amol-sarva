Founder·Investor·Teacher·Writer

Builds companies, backs founders, teaches venture at Columbia, and writes about technology, biology, cities, and making new things people like. A cognitive scientist turned six-time entrepreneur — from Virgin Mobile USA to Knotel to Life Extension Ventures.

Now Life Extension Ventures · teaching at Columbia · New York

IFounder

Six companies and counting — from Virgin Mobile USA’s earliest team to Knotel’s five hundred buildings to a venture firm for people and planet. The habit of a cognitive scientist: build the thing to test the idea.

IIInvestor

Angel in dozens of startups since 2012 — among them JUMP, Marley Spoon, CoverWallet, Goodco, and Hike — and co-founder of Life Extension Ventures, backing AI-accelerated science in longevity and climate.

IIITeacher

Professor at Columbia since 2016. Teaches undergraduates to venture, and runs an advanced studio where students set out to make history by making things.

IVWriter

Essays for HuffPost, Fortune, Salon, and BusinessWeek — on work and productivity, technology and cities, and why some places make more with less.

About

Briefly

Born in New York City. Stuyvesant High School, then Columbia — a BA in philosophy and economics — then Stanford, where a PhD in cognitive science produced a dissertation on The Concept of Modularity in Cognitive Science. A stint at McKinsey followed, and then the first startup did what first startups do: it started everything else.

Along the way: named a Global Vanguard by the World Economic Forum; founded the Wireless Founders Coalition for Innovation in 2007; testified before the US Senate and the FCC on telecom competition.

Now: building and backing at Life Extension Ventures, teaching at Columbia, and writing when the writing is ready.
